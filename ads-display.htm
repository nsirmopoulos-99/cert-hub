Mission Briefing: Mastering the Display Network

The Google Display Network (GDN) is your tool for visual storytelling. It allows you to find the right audience, at the right time, on millions of properties worldwide. This guide focuses on the unique mechanics of "Push Marketing"—creating demand rather than just capturing it.

Exam Concept

The primary goal of Display is Awareness and Consideration. While it can drive sales, the exam focuses on its ability to "keep your brand top-of-mind" while users browse their favorite content.

Core Campaign Types

Standard Display
Manual control over targeting (Audiences/Placements) and bidding. Best for strict brand safety needs.
Performance Max
Goal-based automation. Accesses Display inventory alongside Search/YouTube. Best for maximizing conversions.

Targeting Lab

Targeting is the core of the exam. It splits into "Who" (Audiences) and "Where" (Context). Use the filters below to highlight which targeting methods align with specific marketing goals.

The "Who" (People)

Affinity Audiences

Awareness

Reach people based on long-term passions, habits, and lifestyle.

Ex: "Foodies", "Technophiles".

Demographics

Broad

Age, Gender, Parental Status, Household Income.

In-Market Audiences

Intent

Reach people actively researching or planning to buy right now.

Ex: "In-market for SUVs".

Life Events

Milestones

Targeting around major life moments: Marriage, Moving, Graduation.

Remarketing

Re-engage

Target users who have previously visited your site.

The "Where" (Context)

Topics

Contextual

Target broad categories of websites.

Ex: "All Finance Websites".

Display Keywords

Contextual

Google scans page content for your keywords to place ads. Matches content, not search queries.

Placements

Precise

Target specific URLs, YouTube Channels, or Apps. High control, lower reach.

Advanced Concept: Optimized Targeting. This is a default setting that lets Google find people beyond your signals if the data suggests they will convert. It's like an automated expansion of your targeting.

Bidding & Budgeting Matrix

Choosing the right bid strategy depends on your objective. Use the chart below to visualize where each strategy fits on the Awareness-to-Conversion spectrum.

Awareness: vCPM

Viewable Cost-Per-Thousand. You pay when 50% of the ad is on screen for 1 second. Best for Branding/Visibility.

Efficiency: Target CPA

Cost Per Action. Get as many conversions as possible at a specific cost (e.g., $10 per lead).

Value: Target ROAS

Return On Ad Spend. Get the highest revenue for your spend. (e.g., 400% ROI).

Volume: Maximize Conversions

Spend the entire budget to get the maximum volume of conversions, regardless of CPA.
