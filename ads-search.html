How the Auction Works

Every time a search happens, an auction runs. Google determines if your ad shows and where. The key metric is Ad Rank.

CPC

Cost-Per-Click. What you actually pay. Often less than your max bid if your QS is high.

👁

CTR

Click-Through Rate. (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100. The primary measure of ad relevance.

🏆

Quality Score

Diagnostic tool (1-10). Based on Exp. CTR, Ad Relevance, and Landing Page Exp.

Structure Best Practices

  • ✓ Mirror Your Website: If your site has separate pages for "Running Shoes" and "Hiking Boots", create separate Campaigns or Ad Groups for them.
  • ✓ Thematic Grouping: Tightly theme Ad Groups so the ad copy directly relates to the keywords within it.
  • ✗ Don't: Create a separate campaign for every single keyword (unless specialized). It fragments data.

Smart Bidding (Auction-Time)

Uses machine learning to optimize for conversions in every single auction. Requires Conversion Tracking.

Exam Strategy & Tips

Key philosophies and common traps to pass the certification.

The 4 Core Philosophies

1. Automation is King

If an answer mentions Smart Bidding, Broad Match, or RSAs, it's likely correct.

2. Relevance > Revenue

Google prioritizes user experience. High bids can't fix bad relevance.

3. Value-Based Bidding

Maximize Conversions is good; Target ROAS is better (more mature).

4. Integration

Link Google Ads to GA4 and Google Business Profile for best results.

Avoid These Traps ⚠️

Trap: "Create a separate campaign for every keyword." ▼

Correction: Group keywords thematically in Ad Groups. Fragmentation hurts machine learning data density.

Trap: "Quality Score determines the auction winner." ▼

Correction: Ad Rank determines the winner. Quality Score is just a diagnostic tool (1-10) used to help calculate Ad Rank.

Trap: "Targeting vs. Observation." ▼

Correction:
- Targeting: "Show ads ONLY to this audience."
- Observation: "Show to everyone, but track this audience separately (and bid adjust)."
